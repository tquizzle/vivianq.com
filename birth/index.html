      Vivian's arrival into the world was a much calmer experience than her big brother's was (see SilasQ.com for Silas' story). A C-section was scheduled for 7:00 am on Wednesday, February 25th 2015; Heather kept thinking V might make her debut earlier than that, but Vivian stayed put until it was time for her scheduled arrival. That morning mom was emotional in anticipation of meeting this new addition to the family. Between dad and the two grandma's, she was calmed and comforted.

      Everything went according to plan except for one thing, baby Vivian did not want to come out of the warm and comfy womb she had called home for the past 9 months. The doctor's tried to pull her out three times and were finally successful on the fourth pull. Vivian even tried to turn around and go the other direction in order to avoid an exit. When that happened, the anesthesiologist had to step in and help push her down from the top. The process was a little jarring (literally) but went by quickly and before we knew it, all 9 lbs and 8 oz were out and she was crying loudly in protest.

      Within the hour mom and baby were back in their room cuddling for the first time. Mom had some trouble with nausea and vomiting on that first day but was lucky to have a wonderful nurse named Neva who tended to her and helped her through the sickness. The next couple of days in the hospital were peaceful and without incident and everyone was able to go home on a snowy Saturday afternoon.

      Vivian Lorene? Where's that from?

      The name Vivian means "alive" and mom and dad both quickly agreed that it is a beautiful classic name for our daughter. Her middle name is Lorene which is a name with great sentimental meaning for Heather.

      Heather's grandma on her mother's side was Ida Lorene Linton and everyone called her Lorene. Heather was very close to her grandmother and wanted to honor her by keeping her name in the family. Lorene was a little Indian woman who wasn't loud or outspoken, but whenever she had something to say, everyone listened. She held no punches and always provided Godly wisdom to any family member (or friend) who needed it.

      Lorene would have found great pleasure in her great-granddaughter, and we know that she is smiling down from heaven on this little girl who shares her namesake.
